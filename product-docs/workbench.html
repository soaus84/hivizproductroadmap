Pattern Summary
Six near-misses involving heavy vehicle spotters have occurred across four North Region sites in 28 days. In five of six cases, the spotter was absent or distracted at the moment of the reversing movement — not because of individual negligence, but because spotter assignment and continuity are not systematically managed through the pre-task brief or site traffic management plans.
Likely Systemic Cause
Spotter roles are treated as informal task assignments rather than controlled positions — there is no system that ensures a spotter stays assigned and in position for the duration of a reversing operation.

"Six times in the last month, across four of our sites, a heavy vehicle reversed without someone properly watching its blind zone. Six times. That's not bad luck — that's a system that isn't working.
