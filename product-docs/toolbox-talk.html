Today's Hazard
We're decking on levels 7–9 today. Any unprotected edge over 6 feet is a fall hazard. Wind gusts are forecast at 20–25 mph this afternoon — this affects your balance on open steel and the behavior of materials being hoisted. Falls remain the #1 cause of construction fatalities in the US — 36% of all deaths in the trade.
🔴
Incident to Know
Near-Miss — This Site — March 18, 2026

A worker on Level 5 stepped back while aligning a beam and his heel went off an unguarded deck edge. A coworker grabbed him. He was not tied off. The leading edge at that point had a missing cable section that had not been replaced after crane access. Report every gap in cable systems immediately — don't assume someone else saw it.

✅
What We Do About It
  • Guardrails, safety nets, or personal fall arrest systems (PFAS) required for all work above 6 ft
  • Tie off before you move into position — not after you're already at the edge
  • Inspect your harness and lanyard before putting it on — look for fraying, cracks, missing labels
  • Never clip to an uncertified anchor point — minimum 5,000 lbs capacity required
  • Immediately flag and barricade any gap in the perimeter cable system
  • No work on leading edges alone — buddy rule enforced today due to wind conditions
