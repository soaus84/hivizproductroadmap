Observation taxonomy
Signal Type
AI-determined from observation text and work type context. This is the primary routing field — it determines whether an observation goes to the pool for trend detection or directly to the pipeline. Confidence threshold: 0.70.
barrier_failure
→ Pipeline direct
Barrier Failure
A control that should have prevented harm was absent, bypassed, or failed to function. One event is enough — no threshold required. High severity.
Examples: Excavator reversed without confirmed spotter. Lockout tag removed before isolation confirmed. Guard removed from conveyor during operation.
High priority Single event
unwanted_energy_event
→ Pipeline direct
Unwanted Energy Event
Uncontrolled release of energy that had the potential to cause harm. The barrier may have held but the energy was released in an unintended way.
Examples: Dropped object from height — no injury but landed in work area. Truck rolled slightly before brakes engaged. Hydraulic line sprayed fluid under pressure.
High priority Single event
at_risk_condition
→ Pool
At-Risk Condition
A physical condition or behaviour that increases the probability of harm. The barrier hasn't failed yet but conditions are deteriorating.
Examples: Berm height below standard on haul road. Operator skipping steps in pre-start check. Permit signed before physical hazard check completed.
Pooled Trend detection
weak_signal
→ Pool
Weak Signal
An observation that hints at a systemic issue but doesn't constitute a condition or event on its own. Valuable in aggregation — a pattern of weak signals across a site is meaningful.
Examples: Supervisor seemed uncertain about procedure. Crew appeared fatigued at shift start. Communication between operators was unclear but no incident occurred.
Pooled Trend detection
positive_performance
→ Pool
Positive Performance
Observed behaviour or condition that demonstrates safety practice being done well. Feeds into Resilient maturity signal and can surface in toolbox talks as positive reinforcement.
Examples: Crew voluntarily waited for replacement spotter — didn't pressure to proceed. Supervisor spotted berm issue and actioned before work started. LOTO buddy-check completed correctly without prompting.
Analytics Resilient signal
emerging_pattern
→ Pipeline direct
Emerging Pattern
AI-only signal type. Generated when an observation in context of recent site history constitutes a recognisable pattern — not assigned from a single observation, fired by the trend algorithm.
Note: This signal type is never assigned directly by observation enrichment. It is the output of the trend detection pipeline when a threshold is crossed.
AI-only System generated
Observation taxonomy
Energy Type
The type of physical energy that was present or released. Used to classify the nature of the hazard and drive control verification in V5. AI-determined at confidence ≥ 0.70.
kinetic
Kinetic Energy
Energy of moving objects. The most common energy type in mining and construction — vehicles, falling objects, moving machinery, projectiles.
Examples: Moving vehicle, dropped tools, flying debris, conveyor belt, rotating equipment.
High frequency
gravitational
Gravitational Potential Energy
Energy from height and mass. Stored in elevated objects, people working at height, steep terrain, unstable structures.
Examples: Working at height, suspended loads, unstable berm, excavation walls, stockpile slopes.
High frequency
electrical
Electrical Energy
Energy from electrical systems. Includes both direct electrocution risk and secondary hazards (fire, arc flash).
Examples: Cable strike, exposed wiring, working near live equipment, inadequate isolation, arc flash risk.
Catastrophic risk
thermal
Thermal Energy
Heat energy — both extreme heat and cold. Includes environmental heat, fire, molten materials, cryogenic substances.
Examples: Hot work without PPE, heat stress, molten metal exposure, fire near flammables, cryogenic spill.
Moderate frequency
chemical
Chemical Energy
Energy released through chemical reactions. Includes toxic exposure, flammable substances, reactive materials, and biological hazards.
Examples: Reagent spill, fuel leak, explosive precursor exposure, silica dust, asbestos.
Context-dependent
pressure
Pressure Energy
Stored energy in pressurised systems. Sudden release can cause high-velocity projectiles, structural failure, or blast.
Examples: Hydraulic line failure, tyre blow-out, pressurised vessel fault, pneumatic line disconnection.
Moderate frequency
noise_vibration
Noise / Vibration
Acoustic and mechanical vibration energy. Primary occupational health risk — hearing loss, hand-arm vibration, whole-body vibration.
Examples: Sustained heavy equipment exposure without hearing protection, excessive vibration from tools.
OHS focus
none
No Energy Hazard
The observation does not involve a physical energy type — typically systemic, procedural, or behavioural observations where the concern is organisational rather than physical.
Examples: Documentation gap, communication failure, positive practice observation, investigation process concern.
No physical hazard
Observation taxonomy
Energy Release Potential
The severity of harm that would result if the energy were fully released without control. AI-assessed against the work type context and energy type.
catastrophic
Catastrophic
Potential for fatality or permanent incapacitation. Includes all situations where the credible worst case is death.
Examples: Vehicle strike, fall from height >3m, electrocution, cable strike, blast exposure.
Fatality risk
high
High
Potential for serious injury requiring hospitalisation. May not be fatal but could result in permanent injury or significant lost time.
Examples: Crush injury from equipment, serious burn, significant fall, chemical exposure requiring treatment.
Serious injury
moderate
Moderate
Potential for injury requiring medical treatment. Likely to result in restricted work or medical treatment case.
Examples: Minor crush, strain, minor burn, slip/trip with injury potential, small chemical splash.
Medical treatment
low
Low
Potential for minor injury — first aid level. Uncomfortable or painful but unlikely to require more than basic treatment.
Examples: Minor cut, bruise, dust exposure, minor noise exposure, small ergonomic strain.
First aid
none
None
No physical energy present — observation is entirely systemic, procedural, or behavioural.
N/A
Observation taxonomy
Barrier Assessment
The state of the control or barrier relevant to this observation. Together with energy type, this is the most direct indicator of pipeline routing urgency.
barrier_absent
Critical
Barrier Absent
The required control does not exist at all — no procedure, no physical guard, no permit, no supervision. The hazard has no control in place.
Examples: No PTW process exists for this work type. No spotter procedure for reversing operations. Electrical isolation standard not defined.
Pipeline direct
barrier_failed
Critical
Barrier Failed
The control exists and should have applied, but actively failed at the moment of the observation. The system broke down in practice.
Examples: Spotter present but not in position. Permit signed but conditions not checked. LOTO applied incorrectly — isolation not confirmed.
Pipeline direct
barrier_degraded
Elevated
Barrier Degraded
The control exists and is partially functioning, but not to the required standard. The barrier is weakening — not yet failed but heading that way.
Examples: Pre-start checks done but not all steps completed. Spotter present but communication protocol not followed consistently. Heat management plan referenced but enforcement ambiguous.
At-risk
barrier_held
Positive
Barrier Held
The control was tested — something happened that could have caused harm — and the barrier functioned as designed. Positive signal.
Examples: Crew stopped work when spotter unavailable and waited for replacement. Permit correctly refused when conditions changed. LOTO verified before work commenced despite time pressure.
Positive signal
none
No Barrier Applicable
The observation doesn't involve an assessable control — typically systemic or behavioural observations where the concern isn't a specific physical barrier.
N/A
Organisational taxonomy — Forge Works Map®
15-Factor Capacity Framework
The Forge Works Map® classifies observations, insights, and investigations by their organisational capacity dimension — the structural reason this event occurred. Factors are assessed at confidence ≥ 0.70. Multiple factors may apply (max 3, stored as parallel arrays). Each factor has its own rationale field. Never shown to supervisors — used by safety managers and in analytics.
GUIDE
ENABLE
EXECUTE
strategy
Strategy
How safety is positioned in organisational decisions and long-term planning.
management_systems
Management Systems
Whether procedures, standards, and systems are fit for purpose and cover the scenarios encountered.
frontline_workers
Frontline Workers
Capability, knowledge, and empowerment of the people doing the work.
risk_management
Risk Management
How risks are identified, assessed, and controlled — both proactively and reactively.
operational_management
Operational Management
Supervisor and manager behaviour — visibility, decision quality, and enforcement of standards.
communications_coordination
Communications & Coordination
Quality of information exchange and coordination between people and teams in the work context.
senior_leadership
Senior Leadership
Visible commitment and behaviour of senior leaders — what they prioritise and signal.
resource_allocation
Resource Allocation
Whether adequate people, time, equipment, and budget are available for safe work.
work_understanding
Work Understanding
The gap between how work is designed (Work as Imagined) and how it is actually done (Work as Done).
safety_organisation
Safety Organisation
The structure, capability, and independence of the safety function within the organisation.
learning_development
Learning & Development
How training, competency assessment, and lesson learning are applied in practice.
goal_conflict_tradeoffs
Goal Conflict & Tradeoffs
Pressure to prioritise production, schedule, or cost over safety — and how those conflicts are resolved.
monitoring_metrics
Monitoring & Metrics
Whether performance data is collected, meaningful, and used to drive improvement.
contractor_management
Contractor Management
How contractors are selected, inducted, supervised, and held to the same standards as direct employees.
decision_making
Decision Making
Quality of decisions under pressure — including stop-work authority, risk assessment in the moment, and escalation.
Multi-factor classification: All 15 factors are assessed independently. Any factor meeting the 0.70 confidence threshold is stored — up to 3 maximum per observation or insight. Stored as parallel arrays: fw_factors[], fw_domains[], fw_maturity_signals[], fw_confidences[], fw_rationales[]. "It's not this or that, it's this and that" — the threshold is the defence against over-classification.
Organisational taxonomy — Forge Works Map®
Maturity Signal
For each classified Forge Works factor, the AI also signals the apparent maturity level of that factor based on the observation evidence. This is a signal — not a score. It accumulates over time into a capacity profile.
compliant
Compliant
The system or practice exists and is formally in place, but may not reflect how work is actually done. Rules are followed when observed but may not be internalised. The gap between Work as Imagined and Work as Done is present.
leading
Leading
Proactive safety behaviour beyond compliance. People are thinking ahead, identifying risks before they materialise, and driving improvement without being prompted. Systems are well-understood and consistently applied.
resilient
Resilient
The system adapts and recovers well under pressure. Safety is maintained even when conditions are difficult, resources are constrained, or unexpected events occur. The organisation learns and improves from its experience.
Incident taxonomy
Incident Type
The category of reportable event. Determines pipeline routing — all incident types enter the incident pipeline for triage. Minor incidents may also feed the observation pool for periodic trending.
near-miss
Near-Miss
An event where harm was only potential — no injury or damage occurred, but the conditions for harm were present. The distinction from a barrier_failure observation: the person reporting an incident chose to categorise it as an incident, OR the AI routed it as one due to the proximity and severity of the potential harm.
Examples: Vehicle nearly struck a worker who stepped into the path. Overhead load swung close to a person. Explosive misfire that didn't detonate.
No harm
injury
Injury
Physical harm occurred to a person. Severity is classified separately via injury_classification. All injuries enter the incident pipeline regardless of severity.
Examples: Cut requiring stitches, fractured bone, strain, heat illness, chemical burn, hearing damage.
Harm occurred
property-damage
Property Damage
Damage to equipment, infrastructure, or property without injury. May still be significant — a damaged vehicle or structural failure can be costly and indicate systemic issues.
Examples: Equipment collision, fire damage, structural failure, vehicle rollover without injury.
No injury
environmental
Environmental Event
A release or event with environmental impact — spill, contamination, emission, or damage to surrounding environment. May have regulatory notification requirements.
Examples: Chemical spill reaching waterway, dust emission exceeding limits, tailings breach, fuel leak into soil.
May be notifiable
Incident taxonomy
Injury Classification
Standard occupational injury severity classification. Used for statutory reporting and analytics. AI-determined from description — the reporter describes the injury, the platform classifies.
none
No Injury
Incident occurred but no physical harm resulted. Used for near-misses, property damage, and environmental incidents.
No harm
first_aid
First Aid
Minor injury requiring only first aid treatment — no professional medical treatment, no lost time, no work restriction.
Examples: Small cut cleaned and bandaged on site, minor bruise, splinter removal.
Minor
medical_treatment
Medical Treatment Case
Injury requiring professional medical treatment beyond first aid, but worker can return to normal duties. Includes doctor visits, stitches, medication.
Medical
restricted_work
Restricted Work Case
Injury that prevents the worker from performing their full normal duties — modified work or light duties required. Worker is still at work.
Modified duties
lost_time
Lost Time Injury
Injury that results in the worker being unable to work at all — full lost time from the shift or beyond. LTI is a statutory notifiable event in most jurisdictions.
Notifiable
fatality
Fatality
Death of a worker or member of the public resulting from the incident. Always notifiable. Immediately triggers investigation and regulatory engagement.
Always notifiable
Observation taxonomy
Involved Role
The role of the person(s) involved in or central to the observation — not the observer. Used for contractor management signals and analytics. Optional at capture — AI may infer from description.
operator
Operator
Person operating a vehicle, machine, or piece of equipment as their primary task.
employee
Employee
Direct employee of the operating organisation not in an operator role — tradesperson, technician, labourer, supervisor.
subcontractor
Subcontractor
Person employed by a contracted organisation. Subcontractor involvement is a signal that feeds into the contractor_management FW factor analytics.
Contractor signal
visitor
Visitor
Person on site who is not employed in any capacity — regulator, client, member of public, or uninducted visitor.
Observation taxonomy
Work Type
The physical task or work category the observation is about. Selected by the supervisor at capture (the physical context they know) — this is the "what were you doing?" dimension. Combined with the observation description, it forms the diagnostic pair that drives precise AI classification. Optional but strongly encouraged for physical task observations.
heavy_vehicle
Heavy Vehicle Operation
Operation of haul trucks, excavators, dozers, graders, and other heavy mobile plant. The highest-frequency work type in mining — kinetic and gravitational energy dominant.
working_at_height
Working at Height
Any work where a fall from height could result in injury — scaffolding, ladders, elevated platforms, roof work, pit walls.
hot_work
Hot Work
Welding, cutting, grinding, or any work generating sparks or heat near flammable materials. Permit-to-work required in most contexts.
confined_space
Confined Space Entry
Entry into or work within a space with limited egress where atmospheric hazards, engulfment, or entrapment are possible.
electrical
Electrical Work
Work on or near electrical systems, switchboards, cables, or equipment. Isolation and LOTO procedures critical.
blasting
Blasting Operations
Explosive preparation, charging, firing, and post-blast inspection. Specialised regulatory requirements in most jurisdictions.
lifting_rigging
Lifting & Rigging
Crane operations, rigging, and suspended load management. Gravitational energy dominant — dropped object and structural failure risks.
excavation
Excavation & Ground Disturbance
Digging, trenching, and ground disturbance including underground utility risks, wall collapse, and pit wall instability.
loto
Lockout / Tagout
Isolation and de-energisation of plant and equipment for maintenance. Failure of LOTO is one of the highest-consequence barrier failures.
chemical_handling
Chemical Handling
Handling, storing, or transferring hazardous substances including reagents, fuels, and process chemicals.
maintenance
Maintenance & Repair
Planned and unplanned maintenance on equipment, infrastructure, and facilities. Often involves multiple hazard types simultaneously.
general_site
General Site Activity
Observations about site conditions, housekeeping, access, signage, or general behaviours not specific to a work task. Also used for systemic and procedural observations.
